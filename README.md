# Excel-Dashboard-Startup-Funding-Data
This study examines a subset of the Indian startup funding ecosystem from 2020 to 2025 using a structured Excel dashboard with slicers, pivot tables, and KPI cards. The goal was to go beyond surface-level statistics and identify why money moves the way it does — across cities, stages, sectors, and years.

Questions Asked (KPIs)

-How has total startup funding evolved year over year?
-Which cities attract the most startup capital?
-Where does funding concentrate — early or late stage?
-Which sectors are growing and which are declining?
-How large are the deals being made?
-Is the funding funnel healthy from Seed to Series C?
-What drove the 2024 investment rebound?
-Which city punches above its weight by average cheque size?

 Key Insights

-More Deals ≠ More Money — 2023 had the most deals (213) but least capital ($4.0B). Avg cheque size, not deal count, is the real signal of investor conviction.
-Large Pipeline, Narrow Highway — 353 Seed deals collapse to just 95 Series C. Most startups stall before reaching where the capital truly opens up.
-Tech Is Dethroning Consumer — Tech hit 39% sector share in 2025, overtaking Consumer (33%) for the first time — driven by B2B SaaS and Enterprise.
-2024 Rebound Was Top-Heavy — Investment jumped +49.4% YoY, but 21 mega deals drove $4.64B of it. Strip them out and the market looks flat.
-Bengaluru Starts, Others Scale — Leads in early-stage deals (54) but ranks 6th in total capital. Pune dominates avg deal size at $39.3M.
